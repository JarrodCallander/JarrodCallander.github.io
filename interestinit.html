Interest in IT

I had no training in IT when I joined the Navy, but I was trained to become a Communications and Information Systems (CIS) sailor and that is where my love for IT started. Whilst in the Navy I had a variety of responsibilities and slowly progressed in my career until I became a system administrator onboard HMAS Hobart – a first-of-class DDG and Australia’s most capable warship. On Hobart I was responsible for four enclaves, managing hundreds of workstations across a variety of servers, of different classifications and security: supporting over 180 personnel in an operation environment.

In my last year in the Navy I was selected to undertake cyber security training to become a cyber security analyst. This training was hosted by the US Department of Defense Cyber Crime Center and was the most technical training I have completed to date. The nine-month course cemented my desire to work in the cyber security sector for the rest of my professional career. Due to personal reasons I left the Navy to move back to Brisbane where I now work as a Network Analyst at Brisbane City Council’s Cyber Security Operations Centre (CSOC).

At the beginning of 2020 I decided that I should bolster my resume with formal education so I would not be overlooked by potential future employees as I attempt to move up the corporate ladder. I looked at several different courses and originally wanted to apply for a Bachelor of Cyber Security through another university but ultimately decided it would be more beneficial to get a Bachelor of IT and then pursue cyber related post-graduate courses on completion. RMIT’s flexible study options were one of the major deciding factors that ultimately led me to enrol through OUA among other reasons.

Throughout my study journey at RMIT I expect to learn more broad IT concepts that I missed in my previous IT journey – which was mainly focused on administration and security. I would love to learn more about web design as this is a relatively foreign concept to me outside of inspecting and testing webpages for vulnerabilities. I think gaining more knowledge of all other areas of IT would help strengthen my abilities as a cyber security analyst.
